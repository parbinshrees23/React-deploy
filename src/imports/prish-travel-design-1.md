Design a premium travel and views discovery website called "PRISH" — a cinematic, editorial platform inspired by Mountain Lodges of Nepal. Use a clean black and white palette: pure white (#FFFFFF), off-white (#F8F8F6), near-black (#0A0A0A), dark charcoal (#333333), and mid-grey (#999999), with Cormorant Garamond or Playfair Display for all headings and Outfit or DM Sans for body text. The PRISH logo sits fixed in the top-left corner of the navigation bar at all times — set in large bold serif type with wide letter-spacing, always black on white backgrounds and white when overlaid on the dark hero image; it is the primary brand wordmark and must never be centered or moved; navigation links (Journeys, Gallery, Stories, About) are horizontally centered in the nav bar, and utility links (Contact, and a solid black pill button "Book Now") are aligned to the far right; on mobile the nav collapses into a hamburger icon top-right that opens a full-screen black slide-in drawer with large white serif menu items. The first element the user sees after the nav is a prominent full-width search bar — styled as a rounded-rectangle pill with frosted glass and white border when on the dark hero, or a clean bordered rectangle on white sections — containing a magnifier icon on the left, placeholder text "Search destinations, moods, views...", a microphone icon on the right, and a solid black rounded "Search" button inside on the right end; on focus it expands with a soft glow border and drops a white rounded suggestion panel below with rows of thumbnail image, location name, and category tag. The hero section is 100vh full-bleed with the user's own landscape mountain photograph as background at 70% brightness, layered with subtle low-opacity geometric navy/beige/sky-blue shape overlays, a strong bottom-to-black gradient fade, and a centered editorial headline "SEE THE WORLD" in massive italic Cormorant Garamond at 120–160px with sub-line "One breathtaking view at a time" in small-caps below; as the user scrolls down, the hero image parallax-zooms out slowly, the headline dissolves upward, and the bottom fade bleeds into the white section below. All scroll interactions are deliberate: sections fade and slide up as they enter the viewport, photo cards scale subtly on scroll, the nav transitions from transparent-over-hero to solid white with a thin bottom border after 80px of scroll, and a 2px black progress bar runs along the very top of the screen. The "Select Your Journey" section uses an off-white (#F8F8F6) background with a 2-column editorial header — large serif heading left, body text right — followed by three full-bleed portrait-ratio photo cards in a row (Everest, Annapurna, Manang), each with a ghost number (01, 02, 03), region label in small-caps, destination name in large serif, and a borderless "Explore Hikes ↗" text link; clicking a card slides an internal white dropdown panel up over the card listing trek names as clickable rows with → prefix and a ✕ close button. The photo gallery is a 4-column masonry grid on white with 3px gaps and 4px border-radius; hover reveals a bottom gradient overlay with photo name in serif, location, gold star rating, and download count, plus two circular icon buttons (♡ save, ⬇ download) top-right. Clicking any photo opens a full-screen detail panel split 58% image left / 42% white info panel right: left side has the photo, a "← Back" pill button, and ← → arrow navigation; right panel has category in small-caps grey, destination name in large serif, location, divider line, interactive 5-star rating row, description paragraph, hashtag pills, three bordered download tier cards (Web 2MP free / HD 8MP free-with-account / RAW premium), a full-width black download button, and a bordered save button. A cinematic narrative strip is 85vh full black with the user's landscape photos cycling through 4 panels at 50% opacity — each with a ghost number corner, small-caps label, serif heading, body paragraph — navigated with ← → arrows and an "X / 4" counter. The experiences grid is 6 square columns of grayscale photos with name overlays on hover. The stories section is an asymmetric 2-column grid (1.35fr + 1fr) with the first card spanning 2 rows — full photo with gradient overlay, category, serif headline, excerpt, and "Read Full Story →" link; clicking opens a full-screen story reader with back button, hero image, author, date, and long-form body. The stats band is full black with 4 large Cormorant Garamond numerals (24K photos, 180 destinations, 4.9M downloads, 98% satisfaction) and small-caps grey labels. The press section is off-white with a 2-column grid of white cards: publication name, italic serif quote, author. The newsletter section is full black with centered white serif heading, grey body, and a joined email input + white submit button. The footer is white with a thin top border, 4-column layout (PRISH wordmark top-left of footer + about text / Mountains / Coming Soon / Company links), and a bottom strip with copyright left and social links right. The PRISH AI bot is a floating black rounded pill button bottom-right labelled "🤖 Ask PRISH" that slides up a 360px white chat panel with grey header showing bot avatar, name "PRISH Guide", green online dot, white message feed (off-white bot bubbles, black user bubbles), bordered quick-reply chip buttons, and a grey input bar with a black send button. Include floating buttons stacked above the bot: a white "✉ Enquire" pill and a green WhatsApp circle. Design all screens at 1440px desktop, 768px tablet, and 375px mobile — the PRISH logo always top-left, search always prominent, and the overall aesthetic is luxury editorial minimalism — a high-end travel magazine brought to life on screen.
